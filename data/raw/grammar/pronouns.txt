Q: What are the personal pronouns in Hirnu?
A: Hirnu uses short and simple personal pronouns: ek (I), du (you), han (he/she/it) in singular; ekir (we), duir (you all), hanir (they) in plural.

Q: How are pronouns conjugated for person and number?
A: First person: ek (singular), ekir (plural); Second person: du (singular), duir (plural); Third person: han (singular), hanir (plural).

Q: Do pronouns have different forms for case?
A: No, pronouns remain in the same form regardless of their grammatical function in the sentence.

Q: Does the third person pronoun distinguish gender?
A: No, "han" is used for all genders (he/she/it). Hirnu does not distinguish grammatical gender in pronouns.
