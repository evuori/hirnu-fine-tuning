# Hirnu Language Summary

## Overview
Hirnu is a constructed language with simple and harmonious phonetics, flexible grammar based on particles and word order, and a poetic quality that allows for expressive variation.

---

## 1. Phonetics

### 1.1 Vowels
- **Inventory**: a, e, i, o, u
- **Pronunciation**: All vowels are pronounced long and simple, without diphthongs
- **Quality**: Clean and clear

### 1.2 Consonants
- **Inventory**: h, l, n, r, s, k, g, m, b
- **Quality**: Soft and easy to pronounce
- **Common combinations**: sk, gr, br, ln (used to create rhythm)

### 1.3 Phonotactics
- Vowels and consonants alternate to create a balanced rhythm
- Simple and harmonious pronunciation system

### 1.4 Stress
- **Default**: First syllable stress
- **Examples**:
  - **halin** (to walk)
  - **starnir** (to stars)
- **Poetic variation**: Stress may vary according to the rhythm of expression

---

## 2. Grammar

### 2.1 Nouns

#### Basic Form
- Nouns are simple in their basic form
- No grammatical gender
- No case inflections

#### Plural Formation
- **Regular plural**: Add suffix **-ir**
- **Examples**:
  - grim → grimir (man → men)
  - starn → starnir (star → stars)
  - himrin → himrinir (sky → heavens)
  - barn → barnir (child → children)

### 2.2 Pronouns

#### Personal Pronouns

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 1st    | ek       | ekir   |
| 2nd    | du       | duir   |
| 3rd    | han      | hanir  |

#### Characteristics
- Short and simple
- No gender distinction (han = he/she/it)
- No case variations
- Used for people, objects, and abstract concepts

#### Examples
- "Ek halin hålin." = "I walk in the dark."
- "Du skal lugnin starnir." = "You will look at the stars."
- "Han skirr nu." = "He/She shines now."

### 2.3 Verbs

#### Infinitive
- Verbs end in **-in**, **-en**, or **-an**
- **Examples**:
  - halin = to walk
  - skirin = to shine
  - lugnin = to look

#### Tenses and Moods

##### Present Tense
- Use the basic verb form without additional particles
- **Example**: "Grim halin hålin." = "A man walks in the dark."

##### Future Tense
- Expressed with particle **skal** before the verb
- **Example**: "Grim skal halin hålin." = "A man will walk in the dark."

##### Conditional Mood
- Formed with ending **-skan**
- **Example**: "I den mörka halskan, skimrar stjärnor." = "In the dark [conditional]"

##### Passive Voice
- No separate passive form
- Expressed by word order or context
- **Example**: "Himrin skirr." = "The sky is shining." / "The sky is being illuminated."

#### Conjugation
- Verbs remain in basic form
- No person/number conjugations
- Tenses/moods expressed through particles or endings

### 2.4 Particles

Hirnu uses particles instead of case inflections to express grammatical relationships.

#### Key Particles

| Particle | Meaning | Usage |
|----------|---------|-------|
| var      | where   | Location |
| nu       | now     | Present time |
| ef       | if      | Conditional |
| habr     | by      | Agency/possession |
| skal     | will    | Future tense |

#### Examples
- "Grimir halin var hålin." = "The men walk in the dark."
- "Barn lugnir nu starnir." = "The child looks at the stars now."
- "Ef hålin häbr starnir, grim skal halin." = "If the darkness disappears by the stars, the man will walk."

## 3. Sentence Structure

### 3.1 Basic Word Order
- **Default**: Subject-Verb-Object (SVO)
- **Flexibility**: Word order can change in poetic use or for emphasis

### 3.2 Sentence Types

#### Declarative Sentences
- Simple and straightforward
- Standard SVO order
- **Examples**:
  - "Grim halin hålin." = "The man walks in the dark."
  - "Barn lugnir starnir." = "The child looks at the stars."
  - "Himrin skirr nu." = "The sky is shining now."

#### Interrogative Sentences
- Formed using particles
- **var** for location questions
- **ef** for conditional questions
- **Examples**:
  - "Var grim halin?" = "Where is the man walking?"
  - "Ef hålin häbr?" = "Will the darkness disappear?"
  - "Du lugnir starnir var himrin?" = "Are you looking at the stars in the sky?"

#### Conditional Sentences
- Use particle **ef** (if)
- Can describe future, past, or hypothetical situations
- **Examples**:
  - "Ef grim skal halin hålin, starnir skirr." = "If a man comes walking in the darkness, the stars will shine."
  - "Ef barn lugnir himrin, hålin häbr." = "If a child looks at the sky, the darkness will disappear."
  - "Ef grim halskan hålin, starnir skal skirr." = "If a man were to walk in the darkness, the stars would shine."

#### Imperative Sentences
- Short and concise
- Use verb in basic form without subject
- **Examples**:
  - "Halin hålin!" = "Walk in the darkness!"
  - "Lugnin starnir!" = "Look at the stars!"
  - "Skirin himrin!" = "Shine in the sky!"

#### Poetic Sentences
- **Special feature**: Flexible word order for rhythm and emphasis
- **Examples**:
  - "Nu hålin häbr var himrin, starnir skirr barn lugnir." = "Now the darkness disappears in the sky, the stars shine in the child's eyes."
  - "Himrin skirr hålin, grim lugnir barn var starnir." = "The sky shines in the darkness, the man looks at the child by the stars."

## 5. Example Texts

### Example 1: Poetic Text
```
Nu hålin häbr var himrin,
Barn lugnir starnir skirr.
Ef grim skal halin hålin,
Himrin skirr nu var barn.
```

**Translation:**
```
Now the darkness disappears in the sky,
The child looks at the brilliance of the stars.
If a man comes to walk in the darkness,
The sky now shines for the child.
```

## 6. Key Features of Hirnu

### Simplicity
- No grammatical gender
- No case inflections
- Regular plural formation
- Simple verb system

### Particle-Based Grammar
- Uses particles instead of inflections
- Flexible word order
- Context-dependent meanings

### Phonetic Harmony
- Alternating vowels and consonants
- Easy pronunciation
- Clear, simple vowels
- Soft consonants

### Poetic Flexibility
- Word order can vary for artistic expression
- Stress patterns can change in poetry
- Emphasis through structure rather than inflection

### Analytic Structure
- Minimal inflection
- Grammatical relationships expressed through:
  - Word order
  - Particles
  - Context

---

## 7. Language Classification

**Type**: Analytic/Isolating constructed language
**Word Order**: Primarily SVO (flexible)
**Morphology**: Minimal inflection, particle-based
**Phonology**: CV(C) structure with vowel-consonant alternation
**Purpose**: Poetic and expressive communication
